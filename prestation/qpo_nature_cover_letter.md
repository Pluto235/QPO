# Cover Letter for Nature

Manuscript title: Evidence for transient very-high-energy quasi-periodic activity in Markarian 421 from LHAASO-WCDA monitoring

Dear Editors,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript, "Evidence for transient very-high-energy quasi-periodic activity in Markarian 421 from LHAASO-WCDA monitoring," for consideration in Nature. In this paper, we investigate whether the nearby TeV blazar Markarian 421 shows transient quasi-periodic activity in LHAASO-WCDA monitoring, with Markarian 501, Fermi-LAT gamma-ray light curves, and multiwavelength timing data used as comparison and context.

We believe the manuscript will be of interest to Nature readers because it moves the blazar QPO question into the very-high-energy regime. Most reported QPO candidates in relativistic jets have been identified in Fermi-LAT, optical, or radio light curves. By testing this problem with LHAASO-WCDA, the paper asks whether a similar timing structure can be traced at TeV energies, where the emission is linked to the most energetic particles in the jet. Such a signal would provide a rare observational handle on jet geometry, plasma dynamics, accretion-jet coupling, and possible compact-binary scenarios around a supermassive black hole.

The manuscript also contributes a careful and reusable analysis framework. Rather than presenting a single periodogram peak as a discovery, the study combines continuous-wavelet analysis, weighted-wavelet-z-transform analysis, red-noise surrogate testing, comparison-source checks, and multiwavelength alignment. This design makes the evidence more transparent and makes the workflow easier to verify, reproduce, and extend to other LHAASO and Fermi-LAT sources.

Because Markarian 421 is one of the best-monitored nearby TeV blazars, this analysis should serve as a useful reference for future timing studies with LHAASO, CTA, Fermi-LAT, and coordinated multiwavelength campaigns. We therefore hope you will find the manuscript suitable for publication in Nature, and we would be grateful if it could be sent for peer review.

Thank you for your time and consideration. We look forward to hearing from you.
